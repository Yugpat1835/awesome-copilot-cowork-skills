# Filler Patterns and the So-What Test

The filler pass flags sentences that carry no information specific to this document. These survive every edit because they are never wrong; they are also never useful. Flag them, quote them verbatim, and state which test they fail.

## The so-what test

A sentence fails if either is true:

1. **The transplant test:** the sentence would survive unchanged in a different organisation's document on a different topic. ("Collaboration across teams will be essential to success" fits every project that has ever existed.)
2. **The negation test:** the sentence's opposite is something no serious document would ever say. ("We are committed to quality" fails because "we are indifferent to quality" appears nowhere; an unfalsifiable commitment is decoration, not content.)

A sentence passes if it names something checkable: a number, a date, an owner, a named system, a specific decision or trade-off.

## Patterns to flag on sight

Opening throat-clearing:
- "In today's fast-paced business environment ..."
- "As we all know ..."
- "It is important to note that ..."
- "Needless to say ..."

Unfalsifiable commitments:
- "We remain committed to excellence / quality / our customers."
- "We will continue to monitor the situation."
- "Quality remains our top priority."

Empty mechanisms:
- "... plays a key role in ..."
- "... is a critical enabler of ..."
- "... leverages synergies across ..."
- "... drives value for stakeholders."

Vague quantities standing in for numbers:
- "a number of", "various", "numerous", "significant improvements", "substantial progress", "broad alignment"
- Flag these doubly when a real number exists in an in-scope source: the suggested correction is the actual figure with its citation.

Strategy wallpaper:
- "aligns with our strategic priorities"
- "holistic / end-to-end / best-in-class approach"
- "going forward", "at the end of the day", "win-win"
- "actionable insights", "core competencies", "moving the needle"

Pre-emptive self-praise:
- "robust framework", "world-class team", "cutting-edge solution"
- If the artifact claims robustness, the verdict table should hold the evidence; if it does not, this is filler and an UNSUPPORTED claim at once. Record it in both lists.

## How to record a flag

For each flagged sentence record:

1. The sentence, verbatim, with its location (section and paragraph, or slide).
2. Which test it fails (transplant, negation, or a named pattern above).
3. A suggested correction: either the specific fact that should replace it (with source citation if one exists in scope) or "delete; adds nothing".

## What not to flag

- Topic sentences that are generic but are immediately cashed out with specifics in the next sentence. Flag only if the specifics never arrive.
- Required boilerplate the user identifies as mandated (legal disclaimers, regulatory wording). Note it once as boilerplate and move on.
- Genuine uncertainty stated plainly ("we do not yet know the root cause") is content, not filler. Honesty about gaps passes the negation test.
