# One-pager template

Use this structure exactly. Total body budget: 450 words. The document must fit one page.

## Document title (first line of the document)

DRAFT meeting prep: <meeting title>, <meeting date>

Directly under the title, one metadata line: <date>, <start time> to <end time>, <location or "Teams">, <N> attendees, organised by <organiser name>.

## Section 1: Meeting purpose

Budget: 2 sentences, about 40 words. What this meeting is for and what a good outcome looks like, taken from the invite body and recent threads. If the invite has no stated purpose, infer one from the threads and mark it "(inferred)".

## Section 2: Attendees and what each likely wants

Budget: one bullet per attendee, maximum 15 words each, up to 8 attendees. Format: **Name (role if known):** what they likely want, grounded in a named signal. If there is no signal for an attendee, write "No recent signal". If more than 8 attendees, add a final line: "+N others not profiled."

## Section 3: Open threads and unresolved questions

Budget: up to 5 bullets, maximum 20 words each. Each bullet names the thread topic, who is waiting on whom, and the unresolved question. Lead with anything where the user owes an answer.

## Section 4: Relevant files

Budget: up to 5 bullets, maximum 20 words each. Format: **filename** (owner, last modified date): one-line summary of what it contains and why it matters here.

## Section 5: Suggested talking points

Budget: up to 5 bullets, maximum 15 words each. Concrete things the user can say or ask, ordered by impact. Each must connect to a section 3 thread, a section 4 file or the user's stated focus.

## Section 6: Risks or tensions to be aware of

Budget: up to 3 bullets, maximum 20 words each. Disagreements visible in threads, slipped deadlines, conflicting asks between attendees, or topics that drew friction in Teams. If genuinely none, write "Nothing found in the last 14 days."

## Trim order when over 450 words

1. Files beyond the top 3.
2. Talking points beyond the top 3.
3. Attendee lines for the least relevant attendees (keep the organiser).
4. Open threads beyond the top 3.

Never trim section 1 or section 6 below their minimums (2 sentences and 1 bullet or the "Nothing found" line).

## Formatting rules

- Compact bullets throughout, no numbered lists in the document body.
- No cover page, no table of contents, no images, no tables.
- Section headings as bold lines, not Word Heading styles, to keep vertical space tight.
- Empty sections say exactly "Nothing found in the last 14 days."
